We tend to frame AI literacy as a workforce question: learn the tools, stay
employable. I want to make a different and, I think, more urgent argument —
that AI literacy is becoming a *civic* requirement.

## The franchise depends on comprehension

A citizen who cannot reason about how an algorithm shapes what they see, what
they are offered, and what they are told, is a citizen whose consent is no
longer fully informed. Democracy assumes a public that can evaluate claims.

## Conclusion

If we accept that an informed electorate is the precondition for
self-government, then teaching young people to interrogate these systems is not
enrichment. It is maintenance of the franchise itself.
