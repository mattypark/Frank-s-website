We have built an entire adolescence out of preparation. Practice tests for a
test, which is practice for an application, which is practice for a career that
keeps receding. Somewhere in there, the actual doing got postponed.

## The thing nobody assigns

When you put a sixteen-year-old inside a company that is actually shipping
something, a strange thing happens. The performance drops away. There is no
rubric. There is only the work, and whether it is good.

> Responsibility is the only teacher that doesn't grade on a curve.

That is the bet behind everything I am building: that teenagers handed real
stakes will rise to meet them, and that the rising is the education.

## What I keep seeing

The interns who struggle most are the ones who were told, their whole lives,
that they were exceptional. The ones who thrive are the ones who are simply
curious and unafraid to be bad at something first.
